Privacy Policy

Children's Chess (working title) · last updated 5 July 2026

Short version: the game collects nothing. It runs entirely on your device, sends no data anywhere, has no ads, no analytics, and no tracking — for adults or children.

What the game stores

The game runs completely in your web browser. It makes no network connections while you play. The only thing it saves is your current win streak, kept in your browser's temporary sessionStorage, which is automatically erased when you close the tab. Nothing is sent to us or to anyone else, and there are no accounts to create.

Children's privacy

This game is designed for children and is built to be safe: it collects no personal information from anyone, including children. There is no chat, no login, no advertising, and no third-party tracking inside the game. We comply with the principles of the UK GDPR, COPPA, and Apple's Kids Category rules by simply not collecting data.

Optional email (parents only)

If you choose to join our early-access list, that sign-up is voluntary and intended for a parent or guardian. It is handled by a third-party email service and we use the address only to send occasional updates about the game. You can unsubscribe at any time, and we never sell or share it. Please don't sign a child up.

Links that leave the game

The "Support us" and "Early access" links open third-party sites (for example a tip-jar or email service). Those sites have their own privacy policies; a grown-up should handle any of those pages.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update the date above. Material changes to how any optional email is used would be announced to subscribers.
